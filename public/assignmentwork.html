My simple test: Take away the AI. Does the product still work? If yes, the AI is just decoration. If no, the AI is the product itself. In ailibra, take away the AI and every screen goes blank. That's how you know it's real.

03
Research

Why sales reps hate their CRM

Where a rep's time goes
65% CRM admin
35% actually selling
After every call: log it. Update the deal stage. Write a follow-up email. Prep for the next meeting. Build a report for your manager. The CRM doesn't help you sell — it just makes you do homework.
The root cause
CRMs were built so managers can track reps. Not to help reps sell.

Today: The rep enters data all day. The manager gets nice reports. The rep gets nothing.

What we flipped: AI enters the data. AI writes the emails. AI preps for meetings. The rep just sells. The manager's reports happen automatically.

That's the whole idea behind ailibra.

05
Solutioning

5 ideas that shaped the product

💬
Conversations, not records
You never fill a form. You just say "had a call with Razorpay, they're interested." The AI asks a couple of quick questions, then saves everything — contact, deal, notes, next steps. You talked, it typed.
🎯
AI acts, you approve
When you open the app, the work is already done. Emails are written. Deals are updated. Your day is planned. You just review it, approve it, and go sell.
⏱️
Time-based, not entity-based
Instead of tabs for "Contacts" and "Deals" and "Tasks," the app shows: what's urgent right now → what's coming up next → what you need to do today. It follows your day, not a database structure.
🔮
See only what matters
If a deal is going fine, you don't see it. The AI only shows you things that need your attention: a deal going cold, someone opening your email 4 times, a follow-up you forgot.
🛡️
Demo mode everywhere
Gmail, Calendar, Apollo, and Resend integrations are architected and coded but not live-connected due to billing setup constraints. Demo mode fills in realistic data automatically — 8 seeded emails, enriched contacts, simulated tracking. The product works end-to-end. Connecting real APIs is a configuration step, not a code change.

07
Mode: Ask

/ask — Just ask it anything

This is where you land when you open the app. It's a chat. Ask your CRM anything, and it answers with real data from your pipeline.

How it works

  • Opens with a personal greeting: "Your pipeline is at ₹3.2Cr, 4 deals closing this month"
  • You ask whatever you want, in plain English
  • Behind the scenes, the AI figures out what data it needs
  • It looks up your deals, contacts, activities, pipeline numbers
  • It reads the real data, thinks about it, and gives you an answer
  • You get a clear answer with buttons to take action right from the chat
  • Your past conversations are saved in a sidebar you can open anytime
Example conversation
You: Which deals are at risk this quarter?
AI: Two deals need attention:

1. Razorpay Enterprise (₹45L)
Health: 38. No response in 11 days. Last email about pricing concerns.
[View Deal] [Draft Follow-Up]

2. CRED Platform (₹28L)
Health: 52. Stalled in demo stage for 3 weeks.
[View Deal] [Schedule Meeting]
You: Draft a follow-up to Razorpay
08
Mode: Capture

/capture — Log anything without filling forms

Just type what happened. The AI asks a couple of quick multiple-choice questions (with options from your actual contacts and deals), then saves everything for you.

The flow

1
You type something quick
"Had a call with Razorpay"
2
AI asks a quick question
"Who did you talk to at Razorpay?"
It shows your actual contacts: Arjun (VP Sales) / Priya (Head of Product) / Someone new
3
Maybe one more question
"What was it about?" / "Any follow-up needed?" — quick taps, not typing
4
Done. Everything saved.
Company, contact, deal, call notes, and next steps — all created from one conversation.

Why this is different

Salesforce: Click New Activity → pick Contact from dropdown → pick Deal → fill Subject → fill Description → pick Type → set Date → Save. 8 fields, 2 minutes.

ailibra: "Had a call with Razorpay" → answer 2 MCQs → done. 15 seconds.

The options come from YOUR actual data. If Razorpay has 3 contacts in your CRM, you see those 3 names. If there's an active deal, it gets linked automatically.

Right sidebar shows "Captured (24h)" — a live feed of everything logged today.

→ Health breakdown bars

Contacts grouped by company, expandable. Each shows: name, role, last interaction, sentiment, engagement score.

Contact Detail:
→ AI Relationship Summary
→ All activities with this contact
→ Deals they're connected to
→ Enrichment data (Apollo: role, LinkedIn, phone)
→ [Draft Email] [Schedule Meeting]

Enrichment: When Apollo key is present, auto-fills missing data. Without key, demo mode fills realistic data.

Under the Hood

How the AI actually works

The AI doesn't just guess. It has specific tools it can use to look up your real data before answering.

How every AI call works

  • You send a message
  • AI decides what data it needs (deals, contacts, notes, pipeline numbers)
  • It pulls real data from your database
  • It reads the data and thinks about what to tell you
  • You get a clear answer with buttons to take action
  • Every AI call is tracked so we know what's being used
Tools available
get_deals · get_activities · get_contact
get_pipeline_summary · search_notes
find_or_create_company · log_activity
create_task · ask_user (Capture MCQ)

Loading experience

While the AI thinks, you see fun messages like:

"Reading the room…"
"Spotting the breaks and pitches…"
"Crunching the numbers…"

With a smooth shimmer animation. Never a boring spinner or empty screen. Even the waiting feels like part of the product.
Model
Uses Claude Sonnet 4.6. All AI calls happen on the server — nothing sensitive is exposed to the browser.
